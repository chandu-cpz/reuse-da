Indian Initiatives

Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY)

A flagship government scheme launched by Prime Minister Narendra Modi in 2016, PMUY provides LPG connections to millions of Below Poverty Line households, significantly reducing indoor air pollution and improving public health.

Scheme Info

Launched in 2016 by the Indian government, PMUY aims to provide Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) connections to households below the poverty line (BPL). This initiative seeks to replace traditional cooking fuels like firewood, coal, and dung cakes with cleaner LPG, primarily to improve the health of women and reduce indoor air pollution.

Impact

PMUY has significantly reduced respiratory illnesses and improved public health, especially in rural areas. In Uttar Pradesh villages, respiratory illnesses reportedly declined by 60% after PMUY implementation. Women now have more time for education and income-generating activities as they are relieved from the time-consuming task of collecting traditional fuels. This has led to enhanced quality of life and economic upliftment in local communities.

Benefits
  • Free LPG connection to eligible households
  • Reduced indoor air pollution, leading to better respiratory health
  • Time savings for women, allowing them to engage in other activities
  • Improved quality of life and economic opportunities
Eligibility Criteria
  • Applicant must be an adult woman
  • Applicant must belong to a Below Poverty Line (BPL) household
  • Applicant must not already have an LPG connection in her household
  • The applicant must be listed in the SECC-2011 database or belong to specific categories
Required Documents
  • BPL certificate or ration card
  • Identity proof (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, etc.)
  • Address proof
  • Passport size photograph
  • Bank account details

KUSUM Scheme

An initiative to promote solar energy in agriculture, the KUSUM scheme helps farmers by subsidizing solar pumps and grid-connected solar power projects.

Scheme Info

The Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan (KUSUM) scheme promotes solar energy adoption in the agricultural sector. It has three components: Component-A supports setting up of decentralized ground mounted grid connected renewable power plants; Component-B supports installation of standalone solar powered agriculture pumps; and Component-C supports solarisation of existing grid-connected agriculture pumps.

Impact

The KUSUM scheme has boosted crop yields and reduced energy costs for farmers. In Gujarat, farmers using solar-powered irrigation saw crop yield increases of nearly 30% and energy cost reductions of over 40%. Selling surplus power provides farmers with an additional income stream.

Benefits
  • Subsidized solar pumps, reducing irrigation costs
  • Reduced reliance on diesel for irrigation
  • Additional income through the sale of surplus solar power to the grid
  • Increased agricultural productivity and sustainability
Eligibility Criteria
  • Applicant must be a farmer
  • Must possess cultivable land
  • Eligibility varies based on scheme component (A, B, or C)
  • For solar pumps, existing irrigation systems or new irrigation needs
  • For grid-connected projects, land ownership and grid availability are crucial
Required Documents
  • Land ownership documents
  • Identity proof
  • Address proof
  • Bank account details
  • Details of existing irrigation system (if applicable)

World Wide Initiatives

Sustainable Energy for All (SEforALL)

An international organization that collaborates with governments, businesses, and civil society to ensure universal access to sustainable energy. SEforALL drives global policy reforms and mobilizes financial resources in support of Sustainable Development Goal 7.

Initiative Info

SEforALL is an international organization working to achieve universal access to sustainable energy by 2030, aligning with Sustainable Development Goal 7. It collaborates with governments, businesses, and civil society to drive policy reforms and mobilize finance for sustainable energy solutions globally.

Impact

SEforALL initiatives have mobilized significant funding for sustainable energy projects, particularly in developing regions. In sub‑Saharan Africa, over $50 million has been mobilized for mini‑grid solar projects, expanding electricity access to remote communities.

Benefits
  • Increased access to reliable electricity in underserved communities
  • Job creation in the renewable energy sector
  • Improved community services like education and healthcare
  • Economic growth through support for local businesses
How to Engage
  • For Organizations: Partner with SEforALL through project proposals and funding initiatives
  • For Individuals: Engage with local organizations partnering with SEforALL
  • Participate in community consultations and sustainable energy programs
  • Explore employment opportunities in SEforALL-supported projects

EKOenergy

A renewable energy ecolabel that certifies clean, environmentally friendly electricity. EKOenergy ensures that renewable energy meets extra sustainability criteria while also channeling funds to projects combating energy poverty.

Initiative Info

EKOenergy is a not-for-profit renewable energy ecolabel that certifies electricity produced from renewable sources meeting extra sustainability criteria. The initiative operates Climate and Environmental Funds to support projects combating energy poverty and protecting nature.

Impact

Companies using EKOenergy-certified power have seen up to a 25% increase in renewable project investments. Major corporations like Microsoft and SAP now source EKOenergy-labeled electricity, enhancing their sustainability credentials.

Benefits
  • Enhanced brand credibility for businesses
  • Verified commitment to sustainability
  • Support for energy poverty projects
  • Participation in global sustainable energy movement
How to Participate
  • For Energy Providers: Apply for EKOenergy certification
  • For Businesses: Choose EKOenergy-certified electricity
  • For Consumers: Select energy providers offering EKOenergy options

International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA)

An intergovernmental organization that accelerates the global transition to renewable energy by providing data, policy advice, and capacity-building support. IRENA plays a pivotal role in shaping national renewable strategies.

Initiative Info

IRENA is an intergovernmental organization supporting countries worldwide in their transition to renewable energy. It provides comprehensive data, policy advice, and capacity-building support for implementing sustainable energy solutions.

Impact

IRENA's guidance has helped countries like Morocco and Jordan double their renewable energy capacity within five years, reducing fossil fuel dependence and improving energy security.

Benefits
  • Data-driven insights for energy policy
  • Technical expertise for renewable projects
  • International collaboration opportunities
  • Access to sustainable energy resources
Engagement Options
  • For Governments: Join as member countries
  • For Institutions: Partner on research and projects
  • For Professionals: Access training and resources
  • For Public: Utilize open data and publications

Facts and Figures

The Current State of Global Energy Access

Electricity Access

733 million people don't have access to electricity. Access increased from 73% in 1998 to 90% in 2020, but 675 million people still remain unconnected.

90% Global Access Rate

Investment Need

Between US$35 and US$40 billion are needed annually to attain universal access by 2030.

$40B Annual Investment Required

Renewable Energy

Renewable sources power nearly 30% of energy consumption in the electricity sector, with developing countries experiencing 9.6% annual growth in installation.

30% Renewable Energy Share

Clean Cooking

While progress in clean cooking fuels is evident—with 71% access in 2021 versus 64% in 2015—2.3 billion continue to use unsafe fuels.

71% Clean Cooking Access
