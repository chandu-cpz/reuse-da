Initiatives

Indian Initiatives

Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY)

A flagship government scheme launched by Prime Minister Narendra Modi in 2016, PMUY provides LPG connections to millions of Below Poverty Line households, significantly reducing indoor air pollution and improving public health.

Case Study

In several villages of Uttar Pradesh, PMUY has been transformative. The introduction of LPG connections has resulted in a 60% decline in respiratory illnesses. Women, previously burdened by the laborious task of gathering traditional fuels, now devote more time to education and income-generating activities. Local communities report enhanced quality of life and economic upliftment as cleaner cooking fuel replaces firewood and cow dung.

KUSUM Scheme

An initiative to promote solar energy in agriculture, the KUSUM scheme helps farmers by subsidizing solar pumps and grid-connected solar power projects. This reduces diesel dependency and lowers energy costs, while also providing an additional income stream from surplus electricity sales.

Case Study

In Gujarat, farmers have adopted solar-powered irrigation systems under the KUSUM scheme. These installations have boosted crop yields by nearly 30% and reduced energy costs by over 40%. Moreover, the ability to sell excess power has provided farmers with a steady supplementary income, fostering local sustainable practices and improving overall agricultural productivity.

World Wide Initiatives

Sustainable Energy for All (SEforALL)

An international organization that collaborates with governments, businesses, and civil society to ensure universal access to sustainable energy. SEforALL drives global policy reforms and mobilizes financial resources in support of Sustainable Development Goal 7.

Case Study

In sub‑Saharan Africa, SEforALL's initiatives have mobilized over $50 million for mini‑grid solar projects. These projects have expanded reliable electricity access to remote communities, spurring economic development and job creation. The resulting improvements in education, healthcare, and small business growth underscore the organization's role as a catalyst for sustainable progress.

EKOenergy

A renewable energy ecolabel that certifies clean, environmentally friendly electricity. EKOenergy ensures that renewable energy meets extra sustainability criteria while also channeling funds to projects combating energy poverty.

Case Study

In multiple European markets, businesses that switch to EKOenergy-certified power have seen up to a 25% increase in investments in renewable projects. Major companies like Microsoft and SAP now source EKOenergy-labelled electricity, enhancing brand credibility and reinforcing consumer trust in sustainable practices.

International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA)

An intergovernmental organization that accelerates the global transition to renewable energy by providing data, policy advice, and capacity-building support. IRENA plays a pivotal role in shaping national renewable strategies.

Case Study

IRENA's policy guidance has been vital for countries like Morocco and Jordan, where national renewable capacity has doubled over the last five years. Through its technical expertise and data-driven insights, IRENA has enabled these nations to reduce fossil fuel reliance and improve energy security, thereby contributing to more resilient and sustainable economies.

Facts and Figures

The Current State of Global Energy Access

Electricity Access

733 million people don't have access to electricity. Access increased from 73% in 1998 to 90% in 2020, but 675 million people still remain unconnected.

90% Global Access Rate

Investment Need

Between US$35 and US$40 billion are needed annually to attain universal access by 2030.

$40B Annual Investment Required

Renewable Energy

Renewable sources power nearly 30% of energy consumption in the electricity sector, with developing countries experiencing 9.6% annual growth in installation.

30% Renewable Energy Share

Clean Cooking

While progress in clean cooking fuels is evident—with 71% access in 2021 versus 64% in 2015—2.3 billion continue to use unsafe fuels.

71% Clean Cooking Access
